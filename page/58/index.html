Books Part I: Reference

Get­ting rid of books, even mediocre titles, can be painful. You tell your­self lit­tle fibs like, “Oh man, the Nar­nia series! I think I’m going to read those again.” And you don’t.

I have a few too many books to purge them all at once. Let’s move the big boys first.

A few are even worth a cou­ple coin. Just remem­ber, I’m not ship­ping any items so if you want a book you will have to come pick it up or be so con­ve­niently located that I might be will­ing to drop it off in per­son. Here’s the list:

  • Why Nations Go To War, Ninth Edition
  • The C Pro­gram­ming Lan­guage, 2nd Edi­tion *Taken
  • Explor­ing Social Psy­chol­ogy, 2nd Edition
  • The Long­man Anthol­ogy of World Lit­er­a­ture, Vol­ume E: The Nine­teenth Cen­tury, Sec­ond Edition
  • Ele­ments of Data Compression
  • Philo­sophic Clas­sics Vol­ume III: Mod­ern Phi­los­o­phy, Fourth Edition
  • The Unfin­ished Nation: A Con­cise His­tory of the Amer­i­can Peo­ple, Third Edition
  • Ele­ments of ML Pro­gram­ming, ML97 Edition
  • Intro­duc­tion to Autonomous Mobile Robots *Taken
  • Ora­cle 10g Pro­gram­ming: A Primer
  • Prob­a­bil­ity and Sta­tis­tics for Engi­neer­ing and the Sciences
  • Lan­guages and Machines: An Intro­duc­tion to the The­ory of Com­puter Science
  • Oper­at­ing Sys­tems Design and Imple­men­ta­tion, 3rd Edition
  • Music: An Appre­ci­a­tion, Fourth Brief Edi­tion (No CD)
  • Dig­i­tal Design Essentials
  • Cal­cu­lus, Fourth Edition
  • Biol­ogy Con­cepts and Appli­ca­tions, Fifth Edition
  • Goode’s World Atlas, 20th Edition
  • Invi­ta­tion to Psy­chol­ogy, Sec­ond Edition
  • Native Amer­i­can Architecture
  • Dis­cov­er­ing Bio­log­i­cal Psychology

Happy read­ing folks.
